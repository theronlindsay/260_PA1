Myself — A Student and Builder

✕

I use GenAI as a force multiplier for learning and making things. It helps me brainstorm, draft, and debug—while I stay critical about facts and test results.

  • Generate study plans and flashcards from lecture notes.
  • Turn rough ideas into draft projects I can iterate on.
  • Explain code errors and suggest alternative implementations.
  • Summarize research papers.

Helpful tools: ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Ollama

Tip: Be as specific as possible while prompting, use verbose language to illustrate the desired outcome.

Peers — Classmates and Teammates

✕

Peers lean on GenAI for similar things to myself, Project planning, an idea planner, and as a coding buddy.

  • Draft project charters, timelines, and risk lists.
  • Rewrite assignments for clarity and tone.
  • Create quick mockups and game assets.

Try: Gemini · Figma content plugins

Tip: Make sure to check results for accuracy, and generated images for errors.

Parents — Busy and Curious

✕

Parents might use GenAI to save time and reduce stress for planning, making art, and learning.

  • Plan family trips: routes, budgets, and rainy-day backups.
  • Rewrite emails for clarity and tone.
  • Use AI for general quick searches.
  • Generate images to create art.

Explore: Gemini, ChatGPT ComfyUI

Reminder: Don't paste private data into public AI tools.

Grandparents — Helpful and Accessible

✕

Grandparents can benefit from AI as a friendly helper for access, learning, and staying connected.

  • Summarize long articles in plain language.
  • Generate step-by-step tech help with big fonts.
  • Voice to text for emails and messages.
  • Language translation to chat with friends abroad.
  • General quick searches

Accessibility: iOS Voice Control · Android Accessibility

People who work for the State Government of Idaho

✕

Idaho public servants can leverage GenAI to improve service quality and efficiency while respecting policy and privacy.

  • Draft and simplify public-facing notices, FAQs, and emails.
  • Summarize long policy documents and pull key actions.
  • Create checklists and templates for repeatable processes.
  • Analyze anonymized feedback to spot trends and needs.

Resources: Idaho Office of Information Technology Services · USA.gov AI policy hub

Policy: Follow agency AI guidelines and never input sensitive or personally identifiable information (PII) into public tools.

Small Business Owner — Scrappy and Customer‑Focused

✕

Small businesses can use GenAI to amplify marketing, support, and operations without big budgets.

  • Draft posts, ads, and product descriptions in their brand voice.
  • Answer common customer questions via chat or email templates.
  • Create SOP checklists and training snippets for staff.
  • Summarize reviews to find top fixes and opportunities.

Tools: ChatGPT · Zapier AI · Canva

Tip: Keep a style guide and feed it to the model with each request for consistent tone.
